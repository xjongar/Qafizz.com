About Trust Me Bro

The internet's most confident opinions, ranked.

Every argument on the internet is secretly a tier list. We just made the tier list the point.

What this is

Trust Me Bro is a community site for making and arguing about tier lists — S through D rankings of anything people care about. Fast food. Football keepers. Anime openings. Programming languages. Cereal.

You browse what's trending, vote on where things belong, and argue in the comments. The rankings you see are the community's aggregate opinion, not one person's take, and they shift as people vote.

How it works

  • Browse — the feed sorts by Hot, Top Today, Controversial, or Fresh. Controversial is usually the fun one.
  • Vote — each item shows the community's vote share across tiers, so you see how split people actually are.
  • Argue — comments are threaded, because a good tier list argument has branches.
  • Create — make your own list on any topic and let the internet tell you you're wrong.

Why we built it

Tier lists took over the internet because they're the most honest format for an opinion: you can't hedge. Something is either S tier or it isn't. But most tier lists live and die in a single video or thread, and the argument disappears with them.

We wanted a place where the ranking is permanent, public, and collectively owned — where the argument accumulates instead of evaporating. Where you can come back in six months and see whether the internet changed its mind.

The rules, roughly

  • Strong opinions are the point. Being a jerk about them isn't.
  • No vote manipulation. Multiple accounts to stack a ranking will get the ranking reset and the accounts removed.
  • Don't post anything illegal, hateful, or targeting a private individual. See the Terms for the full version.

Who runs it

Trust Me Bro is an independent project — no publisher, no venture money. The site is supported by advertising, which is why you'll see ads between the lists. That's the whole business model.

Get in touch

Bug reports, feature ideas, takedown requests, or a tier list you think deserves more attention — the contact page has the details. If you're interested in advertising, see Advertise.
